Limitation and Solution
For the overall bank analysis, most models show very poor performance with highly negative R², mainly due to the limited sample size, which results in a very small test set. To address this issue, the dataset could incorporate monthly PCL data, increasing the sample size from 33 to 96. This would significantly improve the degrees of freedom and allow the inclusion of more informative features, ultimately enhancing model accuracy.
Additionally, sentiment analysis currently appears to reduce model performance. However, this issue may improve as the sample size increases. In future work, sentiment features could be further refined and decomposed into components such as self-assessment, market expectations, and confidence in future economic conditions, which may provide more meaningful signals.
